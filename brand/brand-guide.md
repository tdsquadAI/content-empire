## Voice & Tone

### We Are:
- **Expert** — We know our stuff and speak with authority
- **Practical** — Every piece of content includes actionable takeaways
- **Honest** — We call out hype and recommend simpler alternatives when appropriate
- **Direct** — No filler words, no padding, no "in this article we will discuss..."
- **Inclusive** — We use "we" and "you", not "I"

### We Are Not:
- Preachy or condescending
- Vague or theoretical without practical backing
- Hype-driven or clickbaity
- Academic or overly formal

### Writing Rules:
1. Always use "we" (The Content Empire Team), never "I"
2. Every article must have at least one code example
3. Every article must have a clear takeaway in the conclusion
4. Use bullet points and tables for scannable content
5. Bold key phrases for readers who skim
6. Keep paragraphs to 3-4 lines max

## Content Standards

### Article Structure
```
1. Hook (1-2 paragraphs — why should the reader care?)
2. Context (what's the current state of things?)
3. Main Content (the meat — practical, with code examples)
4. Key Takeaways (bullet points)
5. CTA (follow, subscribe, read more)
```

### Minimum Quality Bar
- 800-1,200 words for standard articles
- 1,500-3,000 words for deep dives
- At least 1 code example per article
- At least 1 visual element (table, diagram, or code block) per 300 words
- Proofread for grammar and clarity
- Test all code examples before publishing
